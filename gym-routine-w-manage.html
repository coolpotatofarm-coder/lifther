Tips for Plus-Size Beginners

Things that actually matter.

⚖️
Always Train in Pairs
Every muscle has an opposite. Quads/hamstrings, chest/back, biceps/triceps. Training one side creates imbalances that lead to pain. Use the pair groupings as your guide.
🛡️
Core = Spine Protection
Planks, dead bugs, bird dogs, Pallof presses — these teach your spine to resist force. A stable core makes every other lift safer.
🦺
Protect Your Joints
Start with machines. Never lock out your knees. Slow the lowering phase (3 sec). Wrist wraps help pressing movements if you feel strain.
🧘
Warm Up Every Time
5 min walk or bike. Then 10 bodyweight squats, arm circles, hip circles. Cold muscles tear — non-negotiable.
📸
Track Progress Differently
The scale lies. Check your Weight tab weekly but also track: weights lifted, how clothes fit, energy, and sleep. These tell the real story.
🛌
Rest Is Growth
Muscles grow on rest days. Skipping rest = slower results. Soreness is normal; sharp pain is not. Log rest days — they count.
🥗
Eat Before You Lift
Small meal 60–90 min before: rice + egg, banana + peanut butter, or oats. Fasted training causes muscle breakdown, not fat loss.
🫶
You Belong Here
Every experienced lifter was once a beginner. Go at off-peak hours if crowds bother you. Your body is doing incredible things — honour every rep.
